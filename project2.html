Project details
Why UnicoCampania?

App redesign was the main theme and goal of a solo-project.
It was my first approach at redesign and it was confusing at first and I had to think a lot about the right app I was going to choose as a study subject.
I've decided to use the UnicoCampania (iOS) App for multiple reasons.
First of all, it's an app I daily use, even more times per day, which means I know quite well how the user-flow works and I was able to detect the main flaws in a short time. Another reason is strongly connected to my learning goals, which is why I've asked myself what I wanted to learn by working on this project and with this app. Last but not the least, UnicoCampania, even in its iOS version, doesn't follow the HIG (Human Interface Guidelines) and it's quite far from any Apple native app.

Research and design

After a first phase based on decision-making, my research started. As I mentioned earlier, I knew the app quite well, but I've still decided to explore it in a deeper and broader way. That's how I've realised there were two main call to actions when it comes to ticket: check the ticket you have bought already and buy a new one.

Now, let's move to the issues. The whole app user-flow was confusing in various parts, with different buttons leading to the same action and too many taps required to get simple tasks done, such as just opening your active ticket to make it be checked on the train.
Goal

As a project goal, I've decided to design an app that was able to give you access to your tickets in just one tap.
In addition, 3 tabs can be found and seen in the tab bar: tickets and, the two newly added, parking and sharing.
The first tab the user can see when opening the app is the Tickets tab, where you can buy tickets by choosing the most useful pattern among: finding a solution by inserting a departure and a destination, checking timetables, searching for all the companies affiliated with Unico.

Development

After a couple of lo-fi prototypes and multiple hand-drawn sketches, I've created the first hi-fi protoype of the app, by using Figma as an editing and prototyping software.
I've decided to implement the front-end side of the Tickets tab and that's when the Figma prototype became an app, running on an iPhone, too.
In this first task I gave myself, I had the chance to explore Swift and SwiftUI, using native components and trying to apply what I was learning about the Apple's HIG.
I've used a horizontal scrollview for the "My tickets" section and a list of cards in the "Buy a Ticket" section.
When I've completed this task, I've realised I wanted to do more and to learn more, which is why I've also implemented a full-screen modal that can be openend just by tapping on a bought ticket and that will display all the ticket informations, its state and a QR code.
The QR code is generated from a string (linked to the user) and a random alphanumeric sequence to simulate it uniqueness, by using CoreImage framework. Its functionality is also enhanced by the use of a timer: the code can be refreshed both manually, by tapping the "Refresh" button, or automatically, each time the timer expires.
