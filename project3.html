DriveLink

Project details
The idea

DriveLink allows long-distance drivers to talk with each other, share tips and feel connected while being alone!
With DriveLink, driving for an extensive time becomes a social experience, providing a much-needed break from the monotony and boredom of the open road.
The whole project was inspired by the CB Radio used by truck drivers to communicate with each other to exchange useful informations about roads, traffic and weather - and to have a little company while working, too!

The app concept was validated through a long research phase, which lately became a design and development test-driven process for a team-project.
How it works?

First of all, in order to use the app the user should be logged in. After an onboarding tutorial displayed for the first time only, the user will directly land on a login screen, where it's possible to insert mail and password. If the user has no account yet, it's easy to sign-up just by tapping a link at the bottom of the screen and then filling all the required fields showed in the registration screen.
After this sign-in/sign-up process, it is now possible to use the app. How?
DriveLink works with a match-and-call system, which means that each user has the chance to select some topics of interest and make a call. The system will start looking for a perfect match who is online and ready to talk and shares the same interests or some of them. In the case there are no users available sharing the selected interests, the match will be random to guarantee a working service, even partial.

Testing

During the development process, multiple builds of the app were released on TestFlight, an Apple app specifically aimed at testing.
Both internal and external testing sessions were conducted, focusing on usability, registration flow and localisation. Indeed, the app was localised and with a multi-language support in three languages: Italian, English and Spanish.

Technologies

DriveLink was developed in Xcode, using Swift as a programming language and multiple additional frameworks. We combined SwiftUI and UIKit for the app interface, used CallKit framework as a calling system, experimented with CloudKit for the database and persistence aspect of the app and with Firebase Authentication service for sign-in and sign-up.
However, the app we developed is currently a demo version only.
Making calls has its cost and as far as we looked for a free VoIP service, we had to take a step back, acknowledging our limited resources.
We still had the chance to explore external services such as Twilio and SandBird, although we couldn't use them for a product aimed at being on the market without an appropriate economic support.

Personal contribution

My main tasks revolved around the implementation of the sign-in/sign-up feature with the development of the login and registration screens and the authentication system, adding a multi-language support for localisation and text field checks using regular expressions (RegEx).

A note about safety

As I've mentioned before, the current app version is a demo. Future implementation of the app could include some features or adaptions for the user safety. For safety reason, the call would always be on speaker. If the user has the earphones on, only one of them would be transmitting sounds. Furthermore, a specific car-mode could be implemented in the app, to allow the user to only make calls without having access to any other feature that may be distracting.
Making the app work with Siri could be useful, too, using SiriKit framework, to allow the user to make a call in the app just with a vocal command.
Another possibility would be turning DriveLink into an app working on Apple's CarPlay only.
